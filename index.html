Foreword

The AP Stylebook tells you how to write for newspapers. The Weird Al Stylebook tells you how to avoid being mocked in a four-minute pop parody.

This guide follows the spirit of “Word Crimes”: a mix of legitimate grammar advice, exaggerated pedantry, and theatrical disappointment in humanity.

The Weird Al Stylebook 2

Style Rules

  1. 1

    Apostrophes

    It’s means it is. Its is possessive. If you misuse apostrophes, the internet will judge you silently and forever.

  2. 2

    Less vs. Fewer

    Use fewer for things you can count and less for things you cannot. No grocery store sign has ever obeyed this rule.

  3. 3

    Your vs. You’re

    Your indicates possession. You’re means you are. If replacing the word with “you are” sounds ridiculous, use your.

  4. 4

    Literally

    Use literally when something actually happened in physical reality. If you literally died laughing, please stop emailing.

  5. 5

    Oxford Comma

    Use the Oxford comma whenever clarity matters. Without it, your parents might accidentally become Weird Al and coffee.

  6. 6

    All Caps

    Typing in all caps makes your sentence sound like it was written during a kitchen fire. Use sparingly.

The Weird Al Stylebook 3
  1. 7

    Email Etiquette

    Subject lines should explain reality, contain verbs, and avoid mystery. A subject line of “Hey” is not useful to anyone.

  2. 8

    Final Principles

    Good writing should be clear, concise, human, and readable. Proofread your emails. Use apostrophes responsibly. And never write “for all intensive purposes.”
